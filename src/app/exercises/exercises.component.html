Muscle Strengthening for Knee Recovery

Targeted exercises to rebuild strength, improve stability, and reduce pain during your recovery journey.

Why Muscle Strengthening Matters

Strong muscles around the knee joint play a crucial role in its stability and function. Weak muscles can contribute to increased stress on the knee, leading to pain and discomfort. Strengthening these muscles helps to:

Provide Support

Strong quadriceps, hamstrings, and calf muscles help to stabilize the knee joint during movement and weight-bearing activities.

Absorb Shock

Well-developed muscles act as shock absorbers, reducing the impact forces transmitted through the knee.

Improve Alignment

Balanced muscle strength around the knee helps maintain proper joint alignment, preventing uneven wear and tear.

Enhance Mobility

Improved muscle strength often leads to better range of motion and overall knee function.

Incorporating regular strengthening exercises into your physiotherapy routine is essential for long-term knee health and pain management. Remember to always perform these exercises with proper form and under the guidance of your physiotherapist.

Physiotherapy Exercises

Quadriceps Sets

Beginner

How to do it:

Sit or lie down with your leg straight. Tighten the muscles on the front of your thigh (quadriceps) as much as you can, as if you are trying to straighten your knee fully and lift your heel slightly off the ground (even if it doesn't actually lift). Hold for 5-10 seconds, then relax.

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-15 Hold: 5-10 sec

Straight Leg Raises

Beginner

How to do it:

Lie on your back with one leg bent and the other leg straight. Tighten the quadriceps of your straight leg and slowly lift it off the ground about 6-12 inches, keeping your knee straight. Hold for a few seconds, then slowly lower it back down.

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-15 Hold: 3-5 sec

Hamstring Curls

Beginner

How to do it:

Lie on your stomach with your legs straight. Slowly bend one knee, bringing your heel towards your buttocks. Hold for a few seconds, then slowly lower your leg down.

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-15 Hold: 3-5 sec

Glute Bridges

Beginner

How to do it:

Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Engage your glutes and lift your hips off the floor until your body forms a straight line from your shoulders to your knees. Hold for a few seconds, then slowly lower your hips back down.

Sets: 3 Reps: 10-15 Hold: 3-5 sec

Calf Raises

Beginner

How to do it:

Stand with your feet flat on the floor, shoulder-width apart. You can hold onto a chair or wall for balance. Slowly rise up onto the balls of your feet, lifting your heels off the ground as high as comfortable. Hold for a second, then slowly lower your heels back down.

Sets: 3 Reps: 15-20 Hold: 1-2 sec

Important Note: This is just a sample of exercises. Your physiotherapy program should be tailored to your specific needs and recovery stage. Always consult with your physiotherapist before starting any new exercises or if you experience any pain.
