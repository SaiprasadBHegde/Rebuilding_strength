My Recovery Journey

From the accident to recovery, this is my story of resilience and healing.

October 24, 2024

The Accident

Life took an unexpected turn during a bike accident. Despite wearing protective gear, the impact was severe, resulting in multiple fractures including both femurs, right patella, right tibia and fibula, and wrist.

Initial Surgery

The day after the accident, I underwent surgery to stabilize these fractures. Titanium rods, screws, and a Tension Band Wiring (TBW) technique were used to fix the leg fractures. My wrist was also treated surgically.

October 25, 2024
February 10, 2025

Arthroscopic Arthrolysis

By the end of January 2025, my right knee flexion was severely limited, only reaching a range of 0 to 20 degrees. I underwent arthroscopic arthrolysis to remove the severe adhesions (scar tissue) that had formed within and around my knee joint.

Recovery Milestone

I'm incredibly grateful to have achieved 120 degrees of active knee flexion, a testament to the continuous and dedicated physiotherapy I've been undergoing.

The Crucial Role of Physiotherapy

How dedicated rehabilitation has been key to my recovery journey.

Muscle Strengthening

Building strength in the muscles surrounding the injured areas (quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes, hip, and wrist muscles) is vital for stability, support, and regaining function. Strong muscles help protect the healing bones and joints.

Active Movements

Engaging in active movements of all affected joints, including the knee and wrist, is key to regaining range of motion and control. Initially, for my knee, this involved passive movement with a Continuous Passive Motion (CPM) machine post-surgery, gradually progressing to active exercises.

Overcoming Pain Barriers

While it's important to listen to your body, overcoming the fear of pain and gradually pushing your limits under the guidance of a physiotherapist is crucial for progress. Differentiating between "good pain" (muscle soreness) and "bad pain" (sharp, joint pain) is essential.

Neuromuscular Re-education

This involves retraining the connection between your brain and your muscles. After injury and surgery, these pathways can be disrupted. Exercises that focus on balance, coordination, and controlled movements of all affected limbs help re-establish these connections, leading to better control and stability.

A Message of Hope and Encouragement

If you're reading this and facing a similar journey, please know that recovery is absolutely possible. It requires commitment, consistency with your physiotherapy program, dedicated effort to strengthen your body, and a positive mindset. There will be challenges and setbacks, but by staying persistent, focusing on active movements, and believing in your ability to heal, you can regain your strength and mobility.

Stay consistent, strengthen yourself, and stay positive – you've got this!
